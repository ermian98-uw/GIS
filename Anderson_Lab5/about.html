About Myself

I was born in South Dakota in 1998 and was raised there through 2nd grade. I then lived in Sequim, Washington through 6th grade before moving to Tennessee for 2 years. Ultimately, I moved back to Sequim to finish high school as one of the valedictorians (4.0 GPA).

I earned two bachelor's degrees (B.S. Statistics, B.A. Music) and two minors (Computer Science & GIS) with Honors Program distinction at Western Washington University in Bellingham. To round out my GIS experience and forge a path forward with my statistics knowledge, I decided to enroll in the MSGT (Master of Science in Geospatial Technologies) program at UW-Tacoma for the 2020-2021 academic year.

Alongside school, I currently work full-time at the Tacoma-Pierce County Health Department as a Data Entry Specialist. My department's main responsibility is to aid the COVID-19 Contact & Tracing Response Team by entering vital coronavirus and influenza data for official local and state records. We'll soon be busy recording the coronavirus vaccinations allocated to Pierce County.

I have a multitude of hobbies, but I mostly enjoy playing piano in my spare time. Any genre, any tempo, any era, improvised or notated. Once the pandemic restrictions loosen a little, I intend to play piano locally as I did in Bellingham. Up there, I had a church job, the occasional musical theater gig, tutoring, and vocal/instrument accompaniment work at WWU. I also listen to a lot of music - particularly country music, because it runs deep in my family.
